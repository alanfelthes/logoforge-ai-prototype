Cookie Policy

Last updated: · Version 1.0

This policy describes the prototype’s cookie and browser-storage model and the requirements for a production consent implementation.

1. What cookies and browser storage are

Cookies and similar technologies store or access information on a device. Local storage can also remember security state, checkout status and preferences.

2. Essential technologies

Essential technologies support authentication, fraud prevention, load balancing, checkout session state, language and legal-consent records. Where legally permitted, these operate without optional consent because the requested service cannot function without them.

3. Analytics and marketing

Analytics, advertising and cross-site tracking must remain disabled until the customer provides valid consent where required. The production cookie banner should list each vendor, purpose, duration and category and provide an equally easy rejection option.

4. Payment technologies

Stripe may set technologies needed to prevent fraud and process payments. Link to the current Stripe privacy information in the production policy and accurately describe the configured payment methods.

5. Managing preferences

Users can revisit a “Cookie Settings” control at any time. Withdrawing consent should be as easy as granting it and should not disable essential service functions.
